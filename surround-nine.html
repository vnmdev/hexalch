Surround Nine

A Fill-a-Pix clone — each number = green cells in its 3×3 block (itself + 8 neighbours)
Left-click / drag = green  •  Right-click / drag = red (empty)  •  Middle-click or Erase = clear.
Fill every cell (green or red) to win. Speed mode: paint green only — leftovers auto-fill red when a clue is satisfied, and you win once the greens are right.
→ Hex version (The Great Work)
How puzzle generation works (the logic)

1. Random solution. A grid is filled randomly (each cell green/empty by the chosen density). This is the secret answer.

2. Compute every clue. For each cell, count green cells in its 3×3 block (including itself). That number is its clue.

3. Greedy clue removal under a logic solver. Clues are removed one by one and kept removed only while a deduction-only solver can still rebuild the whole grid. Because complete forced deduction admits exactly one answer, the result is unique and solvable without guessing.
